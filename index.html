About

Background

I work on machine learning systems that learn internal models of the world. Rather than treating prediction as an end in itself, I am interested in how learned representations can capture the structure of dynamical environments.

My current interests lie in world models, representation learning, and causal structure. These areas intersect with probabilistic generative modeling and model-based reinforcement learning, where learned models must represent not only correlations in data but also the mechanisms that generate it.

Projects

Work

WorldSim
An experimental framework for studying world models in simple dynamical environments. Current work focuses on learning latent dynamics in systems such as Pendulum and CartPole, and investigating how representations learned in one environment may transfer to another. The goal is to probe how neural networks internalize structure in physical systems. More on this coming soon.

nanograd
A minimal automatic differentiation engine built from scratch in Python. Inspired by Andrej Karpathy’s micrograd, this project implements reverse-mode autodiff over a dynamically constructed computation graph and a small neural network library on top. The implementation is being extended with support for tensor broadcasting and convolution operations to better understand the mechanics underlying modern deep learning frameworks. More on this coming soon.
